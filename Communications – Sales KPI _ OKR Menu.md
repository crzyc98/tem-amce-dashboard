# **Communications – Sales KPI / OKR Menu**

**Team Lead:** Jon  
**Team Scope:** Supports TEM sales, retention, rebid, and new client finals by presenting Fidelity's communications strategy, participant experience, engagement approach, and overall participant value proposition.

## **Recommended Dashboard Positioning**

The Communications – Sales team should primarily ladder to:

1. **Growth & Retention** — strongest alignment  
2. **Participant Engagement** — secondary alignment  
3. **Scale & Resiliency** — support measure, especially around repeatable sales/finals materials and process consistency

Given the small team size, I would recommend **3–5 core KPIs** only, with several optional qualitative or diagnostic measures.

## **Core KPI Menu**

### **1\. Finals / Rebid Win Rate**

**Focus Area:** Growth & Retention  
**KPI Type:** Outcome  
**Recommended Owner:** Communications – Sales, in partnership with Sales leadership  
**Definition:**  
Percentage of formal finalist/rebid opportunities supported by Communications – Sales that result in a TEM win or retention.  
**2026 Goal:**  
42% win rate  
**Why it matters:**  
This is the clearest outcome metric tied to TEM market share and retention. It reflects the team's contribution to competitive sales and client retention moments where participant communications and experience are part of the decision narrative.  
**Recommended dashboard treatment:**  
Core KPI. Show actual vs. 42% target, with quarterly trend.

### **2\. Supported Finals / Rebid Opportunity Volume**

**Focus Area:** Growth & Retention  
**KPI Type:** Activity / Demand  
**Definition:**  
Number of formal finalist, rebid, or new client finals meetings supported by the Communications – Sales team.  
**Why it matters:**  
Provides context for win rate and helps leadership understand team capacity, demand, and market coverage.  
**Recommended dashboard treatment:**  
Pair with win rate so performance is not viewed without volume context.

### **3\. TEM Market Share / Retention Goal Contribution**

**Focus Area:** Growth & Retention  
**KPI Type:** Strategic Alignment  
**Definition:**  
Communications – Sales contribution to TEM growth and retention priorities through support of market share expansion, rebid defense, finalist meetings, and client retention opportunities.  
**Why it matters:**  
Jon noted that the team tracks directly against TEM market share and retention goals. This should remain a core strategic alignment measure, even if the actual market share / retention numbers are owned at the broader TEM level.  
**Recommended dashboard treatment:**  
Use as a linked strategic KPI rather than a standalone owned metric. Communications – Sales contributes to the outcome but should not be solely accountable for it.

### **4\. Account Executive / Sales Partner Satisfaction**

**Focus Area:** Scale & Resiliency / Growth & Retention  
**KPI Type:** Qualitative / Experience  
**Definition:**  
Quarterly feedback from AEs, sales partners, or deal teams on the quality, relevance, preparedness, and effectiveness of Communications – Sales support.  
**Why it matters:**  
For a small specialist team, internal partner satisfaction is important because much of the value is consultative, presentation-based, and embedded in sales execution.  
**Recommended dashboard treatment:**  
Include as a supplemental KPI or quarterly pulse, not necessarily a primary enterprise KPI.  
**Potential measurement options:**

* AE satisfaction rating after finals support.  
* "Would request support again" score.  
* Qualitative themes from AE feedback.  
* Sales partner comments or testimonials.

### **5\. Participant Experience Story Effectiveness**

**Focus Area:** Participant Engagement  
**KPI Type:** Strategic / Qualitative  
**Definition:**  
Assessment of how effectively the team communicates Fidelity's participant experience, engagement strategy, education approach, and communications value proposition in sales and rebid settings.  
**Why it matters:**  
This is the team's unique contribution — translating participant experience into a compelling client-facing sales story.  
**Recommended dashboard treatment:**  
Use as a qualitative proof point, possibly supported by AE feedback, finalist debriefs, or client questions/comments.

**Key Results:**

* Achieve or exceed the **42% collective win rate goal** for supported finalist and rebid opportunities.  
* Support all prioritized TEM formal finals and rebid meetings requiring Communications – Sales participation.  
* Report collective SCC win rate quarterly, with individual-level reporting for Jon and Leticia.  
* Partner with Sales leadership to review win/loss patterns and identify opportunities to strengthen the participant experience story.

**Objective 2: Elevate Fidelity's participant experience story in competitive sales and retention moments.**
