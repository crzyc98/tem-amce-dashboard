# **TEM Communications Consultant KPI / OKR Menu**

## **A. Client Satisfaction & Relationship Quality**

### **1\. Overall Satisfaction with CC**

Track CC-specific client satisfaction using the CSL survey, aligned to the existing scorecard target of **85% Top 2 Box**.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Why it matters:** This is the clearest direct measure of whether CCs are deepening relationships and delivering value to client contacts.

### **2\. Senior Contact NPS / Satisfaction**

Track NPS or satisfaction for senior client contacts, aligned to the scorecard target of **77% for senior contacts**.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Partners:** RM / MD / service team  
**Why it matters:** Senior-contact sentiment is especially important for retention, rebids, and executive sponsor strategies.

### **3\. Client Relationship Health Actions**

Track whether CCs complete documented relationship actions for priority clients — e.g., engagement strategy review, Financial Wellness discussion, FHI follow-up, Fidelity PlanAlign (SM) support, or client-specific participant engagement plan.

**Ownership:** CC-led  
**Partners:** Analytics, RM, Marketing  
**Why it matters:** This bridges satisfaction metrics with actual relationship management behaviors.

## **B. Client Advocacy & Product Adoption**

### **4\. Client Advocacy Solutions Adoption**

Track whether CCs deliver **3 or more CAS items for 90% of clients**, consistent with the current CC scorecard.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Partners:** Marketing, Analytics  
**Why it matters:** CAS adoption is already part of the CC scorecard and aligns directly to AMCE's "Deepen Participant Engagement" focus.

### **5\. Drive-to-Digital Progress by Book**

Track progress against Drive-to-Digital goals for each CC's book of business.

**Ownership:** CC-led  
**Partners:** B2C Marketing, Participant Services  
**Why it matters:** Drive to Digital is also an AMCE scorecard KPI, with Q2 actuals at **$1.0M versus $0.7M target**.

### **6\. SAS Marketing Campaign Activation**

Track SAS Marketing campaigns turned on, promoted, or adopted at the individual client level; the current CC scorecard treats this as a bonus opportunity with up to 5 points.

**Ownership:** CC-led  
**Partners:** B2C Marketing  
**Why it matters:** CCs are an important activation channel for standardized participant campaigns.

### **7\. Product Promotion / Product Adoption Support**

Track CC support for newly available products, enhanced solutions, or point solutions, including Goal Booster, Retirement Income, Retirement Decision, Modernized Financial Wellness Checkup, and Post-Enrollment Onboarding where applicable.

**Ownership:** Shared  
**Partners:** Marketing, Product, Analytics  
**Why it matters:** The CC resource slide identifies these as PY 2026 SAS items and campaign/product opportunities.

## **C. Consultative Participant Engagement**

### **8\. Participant Engagement Goal Achievement by Client**

Track year-over-year increase in **Education Engaged \+ Highly Engaged active participants**, based on individual client goals and the existing scorecard.

**Ownership:** CC-led  
**Partners:** Analytics, Marketing, Events  
**Why it matters:** This is already a core CC scorecard metric and aligns to AMCE's participant engagement KPI, which was **72.0% in PY Q2**.

### **9\. Engage the Unengaged**

Track the percentage of deeply unengaged or unengaged participants who move to any level of engagement, consistent with the CC scorecard.

**Ownership:** Shared  
**Partners:** Analytics, Marketing, Events  
**Why it matters:** AMCE is already tracking "Engaging the Unengaged," with PY Q2 actual at **25% versus 20% target**.

### **10\. Client Engagement Strategy Completion**

Track % of clients with a documented annual participant engagement strategy.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Partners:** Analytics, B2C Marketing, Events  
**Why it matters:** The CC scorecard explicitly expects CCs to develop an engagement strategy for clients.

### **11\. Semiannual Engagement Progress Reviews**

Track % of clients receiving at least semiannual reporting on unengaged participant movement or engagement progress.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Partners:** Analytics  
**Why it matters:** The CC scorecard directs CCs to report progress to clients at least semi-annually.

## **D. Financial Wellness / Fidelity Financial Health Index (FHI) / Storytelling**

### **12\. Financial Wellness Storytelling Coverage**

Track % of clients receiving a Financial Wellness presentation, aligned to the existing **100% target** in the CC scorecard.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Partners:** Analytics, Marketing  
**Why it matters:** This is already a core CC expectation and can be a key channel for FHI, participant engagement, and point-solution adoption.

### **13\. FHI Activation / Client Discussion Rate**

Track % of eligible clients where CCs discuss FHI insights, intervention opportunities, or Financial Wellness next steps.

**Ownership:** CC-led  
**Partners:** Analytics  
**Why it matters:** CCs use FHI materials to drive client engagement and product adoption, while Analytics owns the FHI measurement framework.

### **14\. Financial Wellness Follow-Up Actions**

Track whether Financial Wellness discussions produce a documented next step: campaign activation, event, FHI deep dive, product discussion, participant education strategy, or leadership follow-up.

**Ownership:** Shared  
**Partners:** Analytics, Marketing, Events  
**Why it matters:** This shifts the metric from "presentation delivered" to "client action created."

### **15\. Storytelling Proficiency Completion**

Track completion of the Financial Wellness storytelling upskilling curriculum launching in January 2026, as reflected in the CC scorecard.

**Ownership:** CC-owned / Manager-owned  
**Why it matters:** The CC role increasingly depends on turning analytics into a consultative client narrative.

## **E. Thought Leadership & Insights Activation**

### **16\. Thought Leadership Inclusion in Client Discussions**

Track inclusion of thought leadership in Financial Wellness or client strategy presentations. The CC scorecard already expects TL inclusion in FW presentations for the book of business, with different expectations by market segment.

**Ownership:** CC-led  
**Partners:** Analytics, B2B Marketing, Events  
**Why it matters:** TEM has a robust thought leadership roadmap across physicians, higher ed, Better Outcomes, healthcare, nurses, and industry chronicles.

### **17\. Research-to-Client Conversation Pull-Through**

Track \# of client meetings where CCs use TEM research, FHI, Fidelity PlanAlign (SM), Industry Chronicles, or Better Outcomes content to shape the client discussion.

**Ownership:** Shared  
**Partners:** Analytics, B2B Marketing  
**Why it matters:** This is a better CC-oriented version of "thought leadership usage" because CCs are often the channel through which insights become client action.

### **18\. Client Success Story Submission**

Track promotional-ready client success stories shared with managers for inclusion in leadership updates or story libraries, consistent with the CC scorecard.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Partners:** Communications / Marketing  
**Why it matters:** This supports internal storytelling and helps AMCE show impact without needing formal external case studies.

## **F. Events & Participant Meeting Activation**

### **19\. Event-Driven Engagement Plan**

Track % of priority clients with participant group meeting or event strategy embedded in the annual engagement plan.

**Ownership:** Shared  
**Partners:** Events, CCs, Marketing  
**Why it matters:** Events can be a practical activation channel for Financial Wellness, retirement income, pre-retiree outreach, women, nurses, physicians, faculty, and unengaged participants.

### **20\. Event Follow-Up / Action Capture**

Track whether participant events or plan sponsor events produce follow-up actions: campaign launch, FHI readout, product adoption discussion, NetBenefits action, workshop attendance, or service-team follow-up.

**Ownership:** Events-led / CC-supported  
**Partners:** Analytics, Marketing  
**Why it matters:** This helps connect events to measurable participant or client engagement outcomes.

## **G. Service Team Partnership & Execution Quality**

### **21\. Plan Sponsor Event Participation / Influence**

Track client participation in B2B plan sponsor events, webinars, forums, or industry sessions where CCs drive attendance or follow-up.

**Ownership:** Events-led / CC-supported  
**Partners:** B2B Marketing, Analytics  
**Why it matters:** This can support AMCE's market-leadership and client-engagement objectives.

### **22\. Service Team Feedback**

Track service team and business partner feedback, aligned to the CC scorecard target of **90% Top 2 Box**.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Why it matters:** CCs are expected to effectively work with and support service teams and be responsive to service team partners.

### **23\. CM Feedback / CM Utilization**

Track CM feedback and utilization, aligned to the CC scorecard target of **90% Top 2 Box**.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Why it matters:** This reinforces good-order delivery, production partnership, and efficient use of Communications Managers.

### **24\. Quality / Risk Issue Avoidance**

Track quality/risk incidents, with client-impacting incidents automatically earning zero points under the current scorecard.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Why it matters:** The scorecard emphasizes proofing, proactive deliverable management, avoiding escalations, and reviewing enrollment materials to ensure pieces remain active.

### **25\. PTABs / Timely Billing Compliance**

Track 100% compliance with PTABs closure and timely billing.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Why it matters:** This is part of Organizational Ownership Effectiveness in the CC scorecard.

### **26\. Time Tracker Compliance**

Track weekly time entry compliance by 5 p.m. Monday for the prior week, aligned to the **90% compliance target**.

**Ownership:** CC-owned  
**Why it matters:** This is a basic operational discipline metric already in the scorecard.

3. **Financial Wellness Follow-Up Actions**
