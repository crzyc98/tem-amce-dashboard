## **A. Grow TEM's Business**

### **1\. Analytics Coverage of Strategic Opportunities**

Track % of strategic finals, new sales opportunities, large RFPs, M\&A opportunities, priority prospects, and rebids supported by TEM Analytics.  
**Status:** Near-term / already partially exists  
**Notes:** You already have something like this for new sales and separately for rebids. I'd keep it, but make sure it rolls up cleanly to the AMCE growth and retention story.  
 

### **2\. MV-to-Lead Admin / Exclusive Opportunity Support**

Track \# of higher ed multi-vendor clients assessed for lead-admin or exclusive opportunity, plus associated accessible assets.  
**Status:** Near-term / strategic  
**Notes:** This directly ties to TEM's higher ed growth strategy and the "MV Attack" work noted in the AMCE QBR. \[TEM AMCE K...PY Q2 2026 | PowerPoint\]  
 

### **3\. Analytics-Supported Pipeline / Conversions / Rebids**

Track $ of pipeline, finalist opportunities, conversions, TOA, and rebids where Analytics contributed materially.  
**Status:** Near-term / already partially exists  
**Notes:** You said you have this for new sales and separately for rebids. I'd keep them separate operationally but potentially show them together in leadership materials as "analytics-supported growth and defense."  
 

## **B. Client Retention**

### **5\. At-Risk / Rebid Client Analytics Coverage**

Track % of at-risk, rebid, executive sponsor, or Client Day priority accounts with a current analytics evidence pack.  
**Status:** Near-term / strong fit  
**Notes:** This aligns very well to the AMCE QBR focus on predictive modeling, Client Day reviews, high-priority relationships, HE MV clients, and HC M\&A risk. \[TEM AMCE K...PY Q2 2026 | PowerPoint\]  
 

### **6\. Client Risk Model Adoption**

Track how often the retention/risk model is used in monthly Client Day reviews, RM/MD conversations, or leadership reviews.  
**Status:** Near-term / strong fit  
**Notes:** This is important because it proves the model is not just built — it is embedded in the operating rhythm.  
 

### **7\. Risk Model Actionability**

Track % of model-flagged clients that result in a documented action — executive sponsor outreach, PlanAlign, FHI, insight deck, consultant discussion, RM follow-up, or relationship intervention.  
**Status:** Aspirational  
**Notes:** This is different from \#5. \#5 asks, "Did we cover the client?" \#7 asks, "Did the insight change behavior?" I'd include this as an aspiration or 2027 maturity metric.  
 

### **8\. Client Insight-to-Action Rate**

Track % of priority analytics engagements that lead to a client or service-team action within 90-180 days.  
**Status:** Aspirational  
**Notes:** I agree with your instinct: Attribution will be hard, but this is the right direction. It could start as a qualitative tracker before becoming a formal KPI.  
 

## **C. PlanAlign / FHI / Advisory Capabilities**

### **9\. PlanAlign Action Rate**

Track % of PlanAlign readouts that lead to a documented next step, plan design discussion, cost modeling request, consultant conversation, or plan change.  
**Status:** Near-term / build now  
**Notes:** Since you already track something similar for cost models, expanding it to PlanAlign is smart. Your operating plan already has PlanAlign readout volume; this adds outcome orientation. \[TEM AMCE K...PY Q2 2026 | PowerPoint\]  
 

### **10\. FHI Intervention Rate**

Track % of FHI readouts that lead to an intervention plan — targeted communications, financial wellness campaign, plan design discussion, or participant engagement strategy.  
**Status:** New / high value  
**Notes:** This is a great one. FHI is explicitly positioned as a way to identify opportunities, benchmark, and measure progress, so tracking whether it leads to intervention is a natural next step. \[TEM AMCE K...PY Q2 2026 | PowerPoint\], \[TEM Plan S...(005).pptx | PowerPoint\]  
 

### **11\. FHI / PPES Follow-Up Measurement**

Track % of FHI or PPES clients with 3-, 6-, or 12-month follow-up measurement.  
**Status:** New / smart maturity metric  
**Notes:** This would move FHI/PPES from "we delivered the readout" to "we helped measure progress."  
 

### **12\. Plan Design Change Influence**

Track \# of clients that evaluate or implement plan design changes after PlanAlign, Plan Health, benchmarking, cost modeling, or Income Replacement work.  
**Status:** High-value / partly aspirational  
**Notes:** I agree — this is excellent. I'd define it as **evaluate or implement**, not just implement, because implementation can take a long time and depends on client governance cycles.  
 

## **D. Deepen Participant Engagement — Shared with Marketing / Comms**

### **13\. Campaign Measurement Coverage**

Track % of TEM participant campaigns with a defined measurement plan — pre/post, test/control, engagement-tier movement, or action-rate tracking.  
**Status:** Shared with Marketing / Comms  
**Notes:** The AMCE QBR already shows campaign performance for Personalized Actions and Higher Ed Spring Campaigns, including reach, open rate, CTR, TAR, and engagement movement. This suggests a more formal shared measurement framework could be useful. \[TEM AMCE K...PY Q2 2026 | PowerPoint\]  
 

### **14\. Engagement Tier Movement**

Track \# or % of targeted participants moving from Deeply Unengaged / Unengaged to Basic, \-Education, or Highly Engaged.  
**Status:** Shared with Marketing / Comms  
**Notes:** This aligns directly to AMCE participant engagement and "Engaging the Unengaged" KPIs. \[TEM AMCE K...PY Q2 2026 | PowerPoint\]  
 

### **15\. Targeted Segment Lift**

Track engagement or action-rate lift among priority TEM segments — women, nurses, physicians, faculty, pre-retirees, younger workers, or lower-income participants.  
**Status:** Shared with Marketing / Comms  
**Notes:** Analytics can help define the cohorts and measurement, but Marketing/Comms would likely own campaign execution.  
 

### **15B. B2C Paid Media Performance**

Track paid media reach, engagement, click-through, cost per action, account-level engagement, or conversion metrics for TEM participant-facing campaigns.  
**Status:** Marketing-owned / Analytics-informed only  
**Notes:** I'd include this in the broader AMCE operating plan, not necessarily as a TEM Analytics KPI. Your team may help with audience insight, measurement design, or post-campaign analysis, but Marketing should own the KPI.  
 

## **E. Thought Leadership / Research**

### **17\. Thought Leadership Usage in Sales / Retention**

Track \# of sales finals, rebids, consultant conversations, or client meetings where TEM research is used.  
**Status:** Aspirational  
**Notes:** You can't do this yet, but it is worth keeping on the roadmap. It would help connect research to AMCE growth and retention outcomes.  
 

### **18\. Research-to-Action Pull-Through**

Track \# of research pieces that produce a client-facing derivative: sales one-pager, consultant version, webinar, discussion guide, client deck insert, PlanAlign follow-up, or FHI follow-up.  
**Status:** Near-term / strong fit  
**Notes:** This is probably the best thought leadership KPI for your team right now. It is easier to track than usage in sales meetings and reinforces AMCE integration.  
 

### **19\. Research Engagement from Target Accounts**

Track downloads, readership, event attendance, or ABM engagement from priority clients/prospects.  
**Status:** Aspirational / future-state  
**Notes:** You can't do it yet, but it is a good long-term Marketing \+ Analytics \+ digital measurement goal.  
 

## **F. Scale, Efficiency, and Resiliency**

### **20\. Standard Analytics Cycle Time**

Track turnaround time for standard analytics deliverables or client-ready decks.  
**Status:** Future / maybe  
**Notes:** Smart, but only include if you can define the workflow cleanly. Otherwise, keep as a future operational maturity metric.  
 

### **21\. Automation Hours Saved**

Track estimated hours saved through automation for PlanAlign, FHI, templates, benchmarking, self-service, or campaign measurement.  
**Status:** Near-term / already trying  
**Notes:** This helps support the "scale and resiliency" story and fills the gap in your current operating plan where "reduce effort" is still TBD. \[TEM AMCE K...PY Q2 2026 | PowerPoint\]  
 

### **22\. Self-Service Deflection / Adoption**

Track % of standard requests handled through governed self-service rather than manual team support.  
**Status:** Future / needs definition  
**Notes:** Agree this sounds great but is hard if you don't know how to measure deflection. You could start with adoption and repeat usage before trying to estimate deflection.  
 

### **24\. Governance / Versioning Completeness**

Track % of key analytics products with documented methodology, data lineage, versioning, claims language, and review status.  
**Status:** Near-term / smart  
**Notes:** I'd include this, especially for FHI, PlanAlign, AI/LLM-enabled tools, and core client templates. It gives you a quality and risk-management KPI without needing defect-rate tracking.  
 

## **G. Internal Enablement**

### **25\. CC / MD Enablement on Analytics Capabilities**

Track % of CCs/MDs trained or enabled on PlanAlign, FHI, research assets, Plan Health, or engagement analytics.  
**Status:** Near-term / okay  
**Notes:** I'd keep this lightweight. You don't want to over-own field training, but it is useful for major capability launches.  
 

### **26\. Field Satisfaction with Analytics Support**

Track usefulness or satisfaction ratings from MDs, CCs, Sales, CSMs, or Marketing partners.  
**Status:** Optional pilot  
**My view:** Yes, I do think you should try it — but very lightly. Not a big formal survey. Maybe a quarterly pulse or post-engagement "Was this useful?" rating.  
A simple version:

* "Did this analytics support help advance the client conversation?"  
* "Was the insight actionable?"  
* "Would you use this again?"  
* 1–5 rating plus optional comment.

This could become a strong internal Voice of Customer metric, but I'd avoid making it too heavy.  
 

### **27\. Repeat Usage by Field Teams**

Track \# or % of CCs/MDs who repeatedly use analytics tools or request strategic support.  
**Status:** Smart / near-term if data exists  
**Notes:** This is better than one-time adoption. It shows whether Analytics is embedded in how the field works.

## **My Suggested Operating Plan Labeling**

If you want to make this clean inside the operating plan, I'd label each KPI with one of four ownership tags:

* **A — Analytics Owned:** Your team directly owns the metric, production, and reporting.  
* **A+P — Analytics Led / Partner Enabled:** Your team owns methodology and measurement, but partner teams must use or activate the insight.  
* **Shared AMCE:** Joint operating metric across Analytics, Marketing, Comms, and/or Events.  
* **Partner Owned / Analytics Informed:** Do not put on your Analytics scorecard as a primary KPI, but show as a dependency or AMCE partner metric.
