

# **Communications – Implementation KPI / OKR Menu**

**Team Lead:** Jason  
**Function:** Communications – Implementation  
**Primary AMCE Focus Areas:**

* **Growth & Retention:** Supports successful onboarding, conversions, and client confidence  
* **Participant Engagement:** Delivers participant-facing implementation communications  
* **Scale & Resiliency:** Drives standardization, quality, automation, capacity, and risk controls

## **Executive Summary of Function**

Communications – Implementation appears to own the communication strategy, execution, quality, and operational support required to deliver successful client implementations, conversions, plan changes, and related participant communication experiences. The function plays a critical role in ensuring large, visible implementations launch on time, communications are accurate and compliant, clients are satisfied, and scalable processes are in place to support growth without increasing operational risk.

## **Recommended Core KPI Set**

For the AMCE dashboard, I would keep Jason's top-line view to **5–6 metrics**:

### **1\. Strategic Implementation On-Time Launch Rate**

* **Focus Area:** Growth & Retention  
* **Definition:** Percentage of strategic implementation communication projects that launch by the committed go-live date.  
* **Example Target:** ≥95%  
* **Why it matters:** This is likely the strongest executive measure of whether the team is supporting high-risk, high-visibility client implementations effectively.

### **2\. Implementation Client Satisfaction**

* **Focus Area:** Growth & Retention / Participant Engagement  
* **Definition:** Client or internal partner satisfaction score for implementation communication support.  
* **Example Target:** ≥9.5/10  
* **Why it matters:** Captures whether implementation communications are meeting client expectations, not just whether work was completed.

### **3\. Milestone Adherence**

* **Focus Area:** Scale & Resiliency  
* **Definition:** Percentage of implementation communication milestones completed on or before target date.  
* **Example Target:** ≥90%  
* **Why it matters:** Provides an early indicator of delivery risk before a project misses go-live.

### **4\. Quality / QC Pass Rate**

* **Focus Area:** Scale & Resiliency  
* **Definition:** Percentage of communication deliverables passing quality review without material defect or rework.  
* **Example Target:** ≥96.5%  
* **Why it matters:** Implementation communications are client-facing, participant-facing, and risk-sensitive. Quality should be a core dashboard measure.

### **5\. Standardization / Template Utilization Rate**

* **Focus Area:** Scale & Resiliency  
* **Definition:** Percentage of eligible implementation communication projects using approved templates, dynamic kits, standardized content, or repeatable communication models.  
* **Example Target:** ≥75%  
* **Why it matters:** Shows progress toward scalability and reduced dependency on one-off custom work.

### **6\. Team Capacity / Utilization**

* **Focus Area:** Scale & Resiliency  
* **Definition:** Percentage of available team capacity allocated to project delivery, strategic work, or operational support.  
* **Example Target:** 85%–95% utilization  
* **Why it matters:** Helps leadership understand whether the team is appropriately staffed, overextended, or capable of absorbing additional demand.

## **Recommended OKRs**

### **Objective 1: Deliver High-Quality Strategic Implementations**

**Strategic Alignment:** Growth & Retention  
**Purpose:** Ensure high-visibility client implementations and conversions are delivered on time, with strong client confidence and minimal post-launch issues.  
**Key Results:**

* Achieve ≥95% on-time launch rate for strategic implementation communication projects.  
* Complete ≥90% of planned communication milestones on or before target date.  
* Maintain implementation client or internal partner satisfaction of ≥9.5/10.  
* Keep post-launch communication remediation below 2% of projects.  
* Maintain Green status within 30 days of go-live for 100% of strategic implementations.

**Associated KPIs:**

* On-time go-live rate  
* Milestone adherence  
* Client/internal partner satisfaction  
* Post-launch remediation rate  
* Escalation volume

### **Objective 2: Improve Operational Efficiency and Scalability**

**Strategic Alignment:** Scale & Resiliency  
**Purpose:** Reduce manual work, increase reuse, and create more scalable implementation communication delivery models.  
**Key Results:**

* Increase use of standardized templates, dynamic kits, or approved communication models to ≥75% of eligible projects.  
* Reduce average communication development cycle time by 20%.  
* Achieve 90% adoption of agreed project tracking or workflow tools, such as Cloud Coach, for new projects.  
* Deliver measurable annualized savings or capacity benefit through automation, standardization, or vendor optimization.  
* Reduce manual touchpoints in prioritized repeatable communication processes.

**Associated KPIs:**

* Template utilization rate  
* Communication development cycle time  
* Workflow/tool adoption rate  
* Hours saved through automation  
* Cost savings realized

### **Objective 3: Maintain Strong Quality, Compliance, and Risk Controls**

**Strategic Alignment:** Scale & Resiliency  
**Purpose:** Ensure participant and client communications are accurate, timely, compliant, and controlled.  
**Key Results:**

* Achieve ≥96.5% QC pass rate.  
* Reduce expired or outdated communication pieces by 75%.  
* Achieve zero audit findings attributable to implementation communications.  
* Complete 100% of required regulatory or compliance communication reviews on time.  
* Reduce communication rework volume by 20%.

**Associated KPIs:**

* QC pass rate  
* Rework volume  
* Audit findings  
* Expired piece inventory  
* Compliance review completion rate

### **Objective 4: Build Team Capacity, Readiness, and Associate Development**

**Strategic Alignment:** Scale & Resiliency  
**Purpose:** Strengthen the team's ability to meet demand through hiring, development, cross-training, and succession readiness.  
**Key Results:**

* Fill approved roles within target hiring timelines.  
* Achieve 100% completion of assigned development pathways or required learning.  
* Increase cross-training coverage across critical implementation communication processes.  
* Maintain voluntary turnover below company or function average.  
* Identify and develop successors or backups for critical roles and processes.

**Associated KPIs:**

* Time-to-fill  
* Training completion rate  
* Cross-training coverage  
* Voluntary turnover  
* Succession/readiness coverage

### **Objective 5: Enhance Participant Communication Effectiveness**

**Strategic Alignment:** Participant Engagement  
**Purpose:** Improve the reach, clarity, and effectiveness of participant communications during implementations, conversions, and plan changes.  
**Key Results:**

* Increase participant engagement rates across implementation campaigns.  
* Achieve email open and click rates at or above benchmark for targeted implementation communications.  
* Increase attendance or participation in implementation-related education sessions, where applicable.  
* Expand standardized participant communication strategies for pooled, small market, or repeatable client segments.  
* Identify recurring participant communication themes and use them to improve future implementation materials.

**Associated KPIs:**

* Email open rate  
* Email click-through rate  
* Workshop/webinar attendance  
* Participant reach  
* Microsite or digital engagement  
* Participant communication effectiveness themes

### **Objective 6: Manage Capacity and Financial Performance**

**Strategic Alignment:** Scale & Resiliency  
**Purpose:** Ensure the function can meet implementation demand while managing staffing, contractor, design, and vendor costs responsibly.  
**Key Results:**

* Maintain team utilization within agreed operating range, such as 85%–95%.  
* Complete quarterly capacity reviews with actionable staffing or prioritization recommendations.  
* Improve forecast accuracy for resource needs and project demand.  
* Keep project delivery within approved budget for ≥95% of projects.  
* Reduce targeted design, vendor, or production costs through standardization and improved planning.

**Associated KPIs:**

* Team utilization  
* Capacity forecast accuracy  
* Contractor spend  
* Design/vendor cost  
* Budget adherence  
* Quarterly capacity review completion

## **Suggested Dashboard View for Jason**

For the executive AMCE dashboard, I would not include every metric. I'd recommend this tighter set:

1. **Strategic Implementation On-Time Launch Rate**  
2. **Implementation Client / Partner Satisfaction**  
3. **Milestone Adherence**  
4. **QC Pass Rate**  
5. **Template / Standardized Communication Utilization**  
6. **Team Capacity / Utilization**

These six measures give leadership a clean view of whether Communications – Implementation is delivering on the core questions:

* Are strategic implementations launching successfully?  
* Are clients and partners satisfied?  
* Is the work being delivered on time?  
* Is the quality strong?  
* Are we scaling the work?  
* Does the team have enough capacity?
